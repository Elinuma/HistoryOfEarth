The Proterozoic Eon

Proterozoic Eon

This Eon ranges from 2.5 billion years ago to 541 million years ago During which photosynthesis is established among organisms and the central part of the continents starts forming. Due to photosynthesis, the amount of oxygen started to rise but it was quickly absorbed into the minerals.

Cyanobacteria, is a multicellular organism that created the Great Oxygenation Event in which they killed the non-oxygen bacteria by providing an abundance of free oxygen transforming the atmosphere,ocean, and mineral chemistry in the process.

This eon is also where the first glaciation occurred; it's called the Huron Glaciation; it happened 2.1 billion years ago.


Rodinia

During this eon, lithospheric plates are already formed and have started moving which resulted in a supercontinent. The best understood of the supercontinents of the time is Rodinia which formed about 1 billion years ago and broke up by the end of the Proterozoic.


Life Evolves

Early life in the Archean and earlier periods lacks extensive documentation in the fossil record. However, chemical evidence and evolutionary theory suggest that it likely consisted of single-celled photosynthetic organisms like cyanobacteria found in stromatolites. These cyanobacteria played a critical role in producing oxygen through photosynthesis. They are prokaryotes, meaning they are single-celled organisms with simple cells without a nucleus and other organelles.
